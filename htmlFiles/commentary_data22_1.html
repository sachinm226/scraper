2.27pm Thanks, Varun, and a warm hello to everyone else. If India can get close to 300, their bowlers could just create some pressure on this pitch. There's some variations in bounce, but the edges haven't carried to the cordon, and the slowness means you can bowl at the stumps - which the likes of Mohammed Shami and Umesh Yadav enjoy doing - and err on the straight side without being taken for too many runs. Plus there's some help for the spinners as well. The question is, how long can Ashwin and Saha survive? We will see very soon. It's Pat Cummins to start, with two slips, short leg, and gully.

1:57pm, Day 2 Good afternoon, and welcome back! Was that not the best day of cricket you've watched this year? I stuck my neck out and said that on our podcast last evening, and the feeling persists today. Just four or five of the teams' best players constantly neutralising each other until a slight lapse in concentration opened the doors for Australia to edge ahead. We'd all take another first session of 40-odd runs today if it were anything like yesterday's, wouldn't we?

Two people who will take it, for sure, are Wriddhiman Saha and R Ashwin. India's overnight batsmen quietly brought some order during a difficult phase last night. No doubt the focus for them will be towards dragging India to 300. It's a pitch with bounce, and a bit of grip, both factors that go against free-flowing scoring. Get 300 and it pays dividends later in the Test. And that is why Australia will be looking to get an early wicket to expose India's lower order early and wrap them up to give their batsmen some time in the sun. Let's see how this goes. 10.02pm What a day of cricket. Terrific bowling from Australia's five-man attack, but there was some high-quality batting from India's three most experienced batsmen too. At 188 for 3 you would have said India were slightly ahead, but a run-out at the most inopportune moment, with the new ball minutes away, opened the door for Australia, and they barged through with Starc and Hazlewood picking up two quick wickets. Ashwin and Saha, however, have put on a useful 27, and if they can extend this partnership through at least the first half hour tomorrow, India could push towards a reasonable first-innings total. This is an interesting pitch: bouncy - with the odd ball climbing especially steeply - but not particularly quick, with quite a few edges not carrying to the cordon. It might be the sort of pitch India's fast bowlers could enjoy, where they bowl at the stumps rather than hanging it out in the corridor. For now, though, I'd say Australia are the happier side.
